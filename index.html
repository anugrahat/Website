Doctoral Researcher at UC Davis (2021 – Present, Davis, CA)

  • Parallelizable GaMD: Achieved 3.5× sampling speed-up and near-linear GPU scaling by integrating GaMD with Weighted Ensemble via Nvidia MPS, surpassing either method alone for free-energy convergence.
  • Predicting Hotspot Residues in Antigen–Antibody Complexes: Developed a pipeline to identify critical antigen–antibody residue pairs, expediting mutational studies for enhanced antibody engineering.
  • Machine Learning for Enhanced Sampling: Leading an ML-integrated MD project to improve sampling efficiency and accuracy in biomolecular simulations.
  • Ovarian Cancer Antibodies: Determined the impact of glycosylation on FAS epitope shielding, improving therapeutic antibody design.
  • Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency: Demonstrated comparable therapeutic potential of plant-derived recombinant AAT via umbrella sampling.
  • HIV-related GI Damage: Revealed dual binding sites of 10-hydroxy stearic acid on PPAR-α, showing potential in HIV-induced GI injury (Nature review).
